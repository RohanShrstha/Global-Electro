PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) tape, commonly known as electrical tape, is a type of pressure-sensitive tape used to insulate electrical wires and other conductive materials. Made from a durable and flexible plastic material, it provides excellent insulation. The tape is coated with a rubber-based adhesive that ensures strong adhesion to various surfaces. While black is the most common color, PVC tape is available in various colors for phase identification, marking, or decorative purposes. It typically comes in rolls with widths around 0.75 inches (19 mm) and lengths of about 20 meters (66 feet).
